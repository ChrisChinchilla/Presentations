Mental Health

There are mixed options on how much energy the brain uses, it does demand up to 20% of our bodies energy. But it is unproven quite how much difference thinking hard and sustained intellectual activity makes above and beyond the typical energy consumption. Most seem to agree that long periods of mental activity can have an impact on your levels of energy, this may be more from the strain of focus or frustration more than anything else, because sometimes intellectual activity can also be inspiring and awakening.
Technology is amazing, we can accomplish many things with it, but it has also helped us shut out the world around us and get stuck in a filter bubble of people all around the world who share our own opinions. This has been a positive for many of us who have always been slightly more socially awkward, but has reduced our connection to the world directly around us. Working from home - Who works from home? How often do you talk to someone?

Why not try…

Go for a walk

Speak to someone you don't work with

Speak to a stranger

Force social actions

Attend an event

Volunteer

Take a nap

Meditation

These are 'air-lock' ideas to get started getting out your comfort zone and getting your brain nice and exercised and exposed to moments of genius and serendipity. Meetups, co-working etc
inconclusive, but at last try to have an hour before bed where you 'switch off', be that mentally or digitally.

Diet

Following on… Often we eat and drink unhealthily to compensate for tiredness or frustration.
Not as unhealthy as I thought: Club-Mate has 20 mg of caffeine per 100 ml. Club-Mate has a relatively low sugar content of 5 g/100 ml (400ml a day - 4 coffees), and low calories (20 kcal/100 ml of beverage) compared to other beverages such as Coke or most energy drinks.
Hands up who has been to a meet-up this week? Pizza, beer? Not as unhealthy as I thought: Maybe? It depends - Not too much cheese or salt, onions, olives and red peppers all good. Takeaway can be worse than hand made or store. Lets not forget chips, candies, cakes, biscuits.
As a developer we often work long hours and long periods of mental strain, this is tiring, so we look to caffeine and other substances to keep us awake. Others are far worse. Mainly sugar, but also ingredients like glucose syrup, corn syrup, chemicals, hidden sugars.
I like a drink, I am a half australian/English ex-musician, of course I do. We all know the downsides of alcohol, just be careful, especially when it's free and everywhere.
